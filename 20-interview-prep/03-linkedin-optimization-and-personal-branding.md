## The headline — genuinely the single most important, most-read piece of text

```
GENUINELY WEAK: "B.Tech Student at [College]"
GENUINELY STRONG: "AI/ML Engineer | PyTorch, NLP, RAG Systems |
  Self-Taught, Building in Public"
```

Genuinely worth understanding WHY this matters so much — recap file 01's ATS-keyword discussion directly, the SAME underlying mechanism: recruiters SEARCH LinkedIn using keywords, and the headline is genuinely one of the most heavily-weighted fields in that search. A generic "student" headline genuinely fails to surface in searches for "PyTorch," "NLP," or "machine learning engineer."

## "Open to Work" — genuinely worth using deliberately

```
Genuinely worth knowing LinkedIn's "Open to Work" feature has TWO modes:
visible to ALL LinkedIn users (a public, green banner) or visible ONLY
to recruiters (a private signal) -- genuinely worth understanding this
choice explicitly rather than defaulting blindly, since some people
worry a public banner signals to a CURRENT employer, though this is
genuinely less of a real concern for someone still in college like
Soham's specific situation
```
